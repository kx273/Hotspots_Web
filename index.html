Manhattan Crash Hotspots

Crash hotspots are not simply the one with the most crashes but those less safe than “similar” sites as a result of site-specific deficiency. Potential for Safety Improvement (PSI), defined as the expected crash count of one site minus the estimated crash count of “similar” sites, has been used in the safety literature as a measure to identify crash hotspots. The safety effects of exposure indicators (e.g. traffic volume, road length) can be accounted for and thus PSI can capture the portion of crashes which are caused by unobserved site-specific risk factors. We adopt the concept of PSI and propose a new hotspot ranking criterion Weighted Potential for Safety Improvement (WPSI), which considers the different costs of crashes with different injury severity levels. WPSI takes into account two important factors that are generally ignored in hotspot identification, i.e., injury severity and effects of exposure indicators.
